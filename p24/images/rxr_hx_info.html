Adiabatic PFR with feed-product heat exchange - Web Labs at www.ReactorLab.net - by Richard K. Herz

The system inlet flow enters the "cold side" of a heat exchanger, then flows to the inlet of an adiabatic, packed-bed, plug-flow reactor. The reaction is first-order in the reactant and essentially irreversible: equilibrium conversion is specified to be nearly 100% under all conditions. The concentration of reactant A is represented as Ca. Constant fluid density is assumed.

When the reaction enthalpy (heat of reaction) is negatively valued, the reaction is exothermic and heats the fluid, which tends to increase the reaction and energy release rates. The fluid leaving the reactor enters the "hot side" of the heat exchanger. For an exothermic reaction, this heats the inlet flow. This type of system is related to an "autothermal reactor" in which the heat exchanger is integrated with the reactor.

This system has feedback of energy. From the default conditions, change the system inlet T to 340 K. Notice the temperature change flow to the right in the cold side of the heat exchanger, then from left to right in the reactor, then from right to left in the hot side of the exchanger. This the heats the cold side again and the process is repeated again.

This system can exbibit hysteresis. Click the Run button and let the system reach steady state at the default conditions. Then increase the System inlet T from 325 K to 330 K and wait for steady state. Note the relatively low conversion of reactant to product.

Then increase the inlet T to 340 K and wait for steady state. Finally, decrease the inlet T back to the previous 330 K and wait for steady state. Note the relatively high conversion of reactant. Thus, there are multiple steady states at 330 K.
